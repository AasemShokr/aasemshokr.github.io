Monetization in video games Part 1

17 April 2020

Hi Developers,

in this article I'll be talking about several ways you can monetize your game (mobile game specifically)

I'll assume you are using Unity engine, let's be honest guys, it is so popular and offers a lot of tools when it comes to monetization leaving the easy part for you (place ads and earn money!)

of course this isn't limited just to unity, these ad networks that we will discuss I believe they also have SDKs for other engines and maybe for Native game development if required!

when it comes to monetization in games, I believe that it is very crucial to place your ads in a way without making players instantly close your game and leave a bad review for the game saying 'Too many ads!'

trust me that will prevent your game from organically growing in the store, you dont want that i guess.

Why do we need this in video games?

Obvoisly we make games for two reasons.

  • We Love Making Games!
  • We Make Games to make money!

let's stick with the second reason for now.
we make game to make money, so I think you didn't spend about 4-5 months making a game that will not make you money!

I think getting this part done effectively is totally worth it in terms of the revenue it generates when done well.

Revenue Generation ( Monetization in free apps )

Generating Revenue from displaying ads inside some placements in your game is a way of generatig revenue, as advertisers work with ad networks (Ad providers for your game).
basically the way this works is that they pay the network for displaying ads in your game and the network shares that with you depending on the number of ad impressions your game requests wich depends on your DAU (Daily active users).
but all of that can Make you a millionaire or just destroy your game (if not done well).
today I'll discuss with you some of the ad networks out there and the pros and cons from my point of view and my experience with them.

1. Unity Ads

2. Google Admob

AdMob is a Google-owned and operated company that has been conducting business since 2012. They serve over 200 billion ad requests worldwide every month with over 1 million advertisers and have 1 million apps currently running on the AdMob mobile ad network.

Some features include being able to cross-sell other apps for free using house ads, running direct deals with advertisers and auto updates through Google Play services without needing to modify your SDK. Platforms supported include iOS, Android, Unity, and Cocos2d-x.
